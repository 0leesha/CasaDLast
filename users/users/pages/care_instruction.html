Furniture Care Guide

Keep your furniture looking beautiful for years with our comprehensive care instructions and maintenance tips.

Wooden Furniture

Daily Cleaning

Dust regularly with a soft, dry cloth. For deeper cleaning, use a slightly damp cloth followed by immediate drying with a clean, dry cloth to prevent water damage.

Sun Protection

Avoid placing wooden furniture in direct sunlight as it can cause fading and cracking. Use curtains or blinds to protect from harsh UV rays.

Polish & Treatment

Apply high-quality furniture polish or wax every 3-6 months to maintain the wood's luster and create a protective barrier. Always test on a small hidden area first.

DO
  • Use coasters and placemats
  • Wipe spills immediately
  • Maintain 40-60% humidity levels
  • Rotate furniture periodically
  • Use felt pads under objects
DON'T
  • Place hot items directly on surface
  • Use harsh chemical cleaners
  • Drag furniture across floors
  • Expose to extreme temperatures
  • Over-wet the wood while cleaning

Upholstered Furniture

Regular Vacuuming

Vacuum upholstery weekly using a soft brush attachment to remove dust, dirt, and pet hair. Pay special attention to crevices and under cushions.

Stain Removal

Blot spills immediately with a clean, white cloth. Don't rub as it can spread the stain. For tough stains, use fabric-specific cleaners or consult a professional.

Cushion Care

Rotate and flip cushions regularly to ensure even wear. Plump cushions daily to maintain their shape and comfort.

DO
  • Test cleaners on hidden areas first
  • Keep pets' nails trimmed
  • Use throws for extra protection
  • Professional cleaning annually
  • Check care labels before cleaning
DON'T
  • Sit on armrests or backs
  • Use bleach or harsh chemicals
  • Over-wet fabric while cleaning
  • Place in direct sunlight
  • Ignore care label instructions

Leather Furniture

Conditioning

Apply leather conditioner every 6-12 months to keep the leather supple and prevent cracking. This helps maintain natural oils and prevents drying.

Gentle Cleaning

Wipe with a soft, slightly damp cloth weekly. For deeper cleaning, use pH-balanced leather cleaner. Dry immediately with a clean cloth.

Protection

Keep leather away from heat sources and direct sunlight. Maintain consistent room temperature and humidity to prevent cracking and fading.

DO
  • Wipe spills immediately
  • Use leather-specific products
  • Allow natural patina to develop
  • Keep away from heat sources
  • Blot moisture, don't rub
DON'T
  • Use soap or detergents
  • Place near radiators or heaters
  • Use colored cloths for cleaning
  • Apply shoe polish or wax
  • Expose to direct sunlight

Metal Furniture

Rust Prevention

Keep metal furniture dry and well-ventilated. Apply protective wax or clear coat annually for outdoor pieces. Address any rust spots immediately.

Cleaning

Clean with mild soap and water solution. Dry thoroughly to prevent water spots and rust. For chrome or stainless steel, use appropriate metal cleaners.

DO
  • Dry immediately after cleaning
  • Cover outdoor furniture when not in use
  • Apply rust-resistant coating
  • Polish chrome regularly
  • Use furniture pads on legs
DON'T
  • Use abrasive cleaners
  • Leave moisture on surface
  • Drag across rough surfaces
  • Use steel wool on finishes
  • Ignore rust spots

Glass Furniture

Streak-Free Cleaning

Use glass cleaner or a solution of water and vinegar. Spray on cloth, not directly on glass, to avoid liquid seeping into joints. Wipe in circular motions.

Scratch Prevention

Always use placemats, coasters, and table pads. Avoid dragging objects across glass surfaces to prevent scratches and chips.

DO
  • Use microfiber cloths
  • Wipe in circular motions
  • Use protective pads
  • Clean regularly to prevent buildup
  • Buff dry for shine
DON'T
  • Use abrasive materials
  • Place very hot items directly
  • Use newspaper for cleaning
  • Apply excessive pressure
  • Use ammonia-based cleaners on tinted glass
